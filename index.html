Date of Death and Estate Appraisals

Date of death appraisals are essential when a property owner passes away. The IRS requires a fair market value opinion as of the date of death to establish the stepped-up tax basis for inherited real estate. We prepare IRS-compliant retrospective appraisals that meet documentation requirements for estate tax returns, probate proceedings, and trust administration. Our retrospective appraisals can document values from decades past, and we have completed thousands of these assignments for estate attorneys, CPAs, executors, and trustees throughout the Coachella Valley region.

⚖

Divorce and Bankruptcy Appraisals

In divorce and marriage dissolution matters, an accurate property valuation is essential for equitable distribution of assets. We handle both current-date and retrospective valuations, maintain strict confidentiality between parties, and offer a two-client option so both spouses can rely on the same impartial appraisal. For bankruptcy proceedings, our appraisals establish property value for the court in a format that is thorough, defensible, and straightforward. We have successfully supported thousands of bankruptcy filings across Chapter 7, Chapter 11, and Chapter 13 cases.

Pre-Purchase and Pre-Sale Appraisals

Considering a home purchase in the Coachella Valley? A pre-purchase appraisal gives you an independent opinion of value before you commit, providing leverage to negotiate a fair price and potentially saving you thousands of dollars. If you are selling, a pre-sale appraisal helps you set an accurate asking price, avoid costly pricing mistakes, and reduce the risk of an appraisal surprise during escrow.
