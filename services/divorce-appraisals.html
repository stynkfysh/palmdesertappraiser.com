Divorce Appraisals in Palm Desert & the Coachella Valley

During a divorce, the family home is often the most valuable asset to divide. A professional divorce appraisal provides a neutral, independent opinion of market value that both parties and the court can rely on. Whether the home will be sold, bought out by one spouse, or retained as part of a settlement agreement, an accurate appraisal is the foundation of a fair property division.

Why a Divorce Appraisal Matters

California is a community property state, meaning assets acquired during marriage are generally split equally. The court needs an objective property value to ensure equitable division. Online estimates and tax assessments are not accepted by family courts because they lack the accuracy and defensibility of a professional appraisal. A certified appraisal protects both parties from overpaying or undervaluing their share of the marital estate.

Two-Client Discount

When both spouses agree to share one appraisal, I offer a two-client discount. Both parties receive a copy of the same independent report, which saves money and simplifies the process. This works well when both sides want a fair valuation without the expense of competing appraisals. If each party prefers their own appraiser, I am equally comfortable working for one side.

What to Expect

The appraisal process begins with a physical inspection of the property, followed by research into comparable sales in your neighborhood. I prepare a detailed written report documenting the property's condition, features, upgrades, and market position. The report is designed to withstand scrutiny from opposing counsel, mediators, and family court judges.

Retroactive Valuations

In some divorce cases, the court requires a valuation as of the date of separation rather than the current date. I have extensive experience with retroactive appraisals, researching market conditions and comparable sales from the specific date needed by the court.

Court Acceptance and Testimony

All divorce appraisals are prepared in compliance with USPAP and are accepted by Riverside County family courts. With over 1,000 divorce appraisals completed throughout the Coachella Valley, I have the experience and credibility that attorneys and judges expect. Expert witness testimony is available if your case goes to trial.
