Estate & Trust Appraisals in Palm Desert & the Coachella Valley

Estate and trust appraisals establish the fair market value of real property for probate settlement, trust distribution to beneficiaries, and trustee accounting purposes. Whether a property is held in a revocable living trust, testamentary trust, or as part of a probate estate, a professional appraisal ensures that all parties—trustees, executors, beneficiaries, and the probate court—have accurate, defensible valuations for their records and distributions.

Who Needs an Estate or Trust Appraisal

Trustees administering revocable living trusts, executors managing probate estates, probate attorneys, CPAs, and beneficiaries all rely on estate appraisals. When a trust directs the sale of real property or the distribution of a home to one beneficiary while cash goes to others, a professional appraisal documents the property's value for equitable distribution and tax reporting. Beneficiary reports to courts and trust documents often require certified valuations to prove the estate was administered fairly and accurately.

How Estate and Trust Appraisals Work

I conduct a thorough inspection of the property, analyze comparable sales in the current market, and determine the fair market value as of the date the appraisal is ordered (or the date specified by the trustee or executor). The appraisal report includes detailed property description, market analysis, comparable sales, and professional opinion of value. This report becomes part of the trust or probate court record and is accepted by banks, title companies, and beneficiaries as proof of valuation for accounting and distribution purposes.

Trust Courts and Probate Court Acceptance

My appraisals comply fully with USPAP standards and are routinely accepted by Riverside County probate courts, trust administrators, and estate attorneys throughout the Coachella Valley. With more than 20 years of experience and thousands of estate and trust appraisals completed, my reports are recognized as reliable and thorough documentation for court filings, beneficiary statements, and trust accounting.

Equitable Beneficiary Distribution

When a trust or will directs that one beneficiary receive real property while others receive liquid assets, an accurate appraisal ensures fair value exchange. If a home worth $500,000 goes to one heir and cash to others, the trust administration must document that everyone received equal or proportional benefit. A credible appraisal protects the trustee from disputes and ensures the distribution is equitable and defensible.
